LAB 3 – Situation Report

TS/SCI – Project [REDACTED]

Event Summary

During a controlled test of Project [REDACTED] by Group B on 2/12, all 16 personnel members [9 ENG, 3 THR, 4 SUP] inside the [REDACTED] simultaneously lost consciousness, triggering immediate shutdown/containment procedures 3.X.1-23,38. Within 3 minutes of LOC, all affected members developed a fever, spiking to 106F, settling within 10 minutes and persisting at 100F until a second spike to 106F occurred 10 minutes prior to ROC on 2/21. Members were catatonic for that duration, assisted to infirmary and provided TPN by drone and 2 MED staff added 2/13. 4 members DNS, in all cases CoD intense fever >109F followed by MI. After brief period of delirium, remaining members are AOx3, VS and labs WNL [UNREDACTED--with exceptions of "exogenous" findings in members AD and VL--UNREDACTED]. Lab 3 remains in full lockdown, all contents in place plus 2 medical team members and 3 mo consumable rations/ 1mo TPN introduced via bivalve. Project [REDACTED] suspended. [UNREDACTED--Project X[TBD] pending--UNREDACTED].

Personnel Account Summary

Following ROC and initial MED exam, several lab members (LM) began recounting a "shared experience" during their catatonic state. All were subsequently separated, individual PSYCH exam and interviews conducted for cross correlation. Individually obtained reports without interference were highly consistent. All describe:

  • No LOC from their POV, all remained in contact and communication with each other
  • An initial experience of "the Object" in a "mathematical" location
  • Unintentional transport to an alien location, with alternate physics and nonhuman advanced life
  • Shared experience of events and interactions in that location, independently reported down to small details
  • Both malevolent and benevolent interactions with local life, the former resulting in all 4 member deaths
  • Receiving training to "travel" to other "worlds" using the Object, allowing a return home

All subsequent sections until Analysis are compiled from LM accounts

The Object

  • Experienced immediately following initial event in Lab 3
  • Both the space and Object described as occuring to LMs "cognitively" in an abstract location
  • All 3 THR LMs state the embedding space is "topologically projective" - that only direction matters, not linear distance
  • All 3 THR LMs state the Object itself is higher‑dimensional and orbifold‑like
  • LMs recount having no physical bodies or movement themselves, only a perceived sense of the Object and other members
  • Following "training," all state the Object can be partially queried/searched, and is used to navigate and travel
  • Some claim the Object has "intentionality" and is not purely mechanical, though others disagree

Transit Process (Meditative Teleportation)

  • Knowledge of this process reportedly learned from alien lifeform, through nonsymbolic telepathy
  • Transit is only possible by having knowledge of the Object, or by an activity that triggers a connection. It is surmised the 2/12 test initiated such a connection
  • Once knowledge obtained, one need only focus intently on the mental re-imagining of the Object, to the point that other thoughts become muted. This connection can be formed at any time, with no energy requirement
  • Subject is then mentally brought back to Object space, where they can "intuitively" search the Object for "world anchors," or snapshots of other worlds that define them. Minimal prior concepts of intended destination world may be necessary for successful searches, as possibilites described as "uncountably infinite".
  • Focusing again on the anchor induces transit to the corresponding world. Transport is instant and projective- distance does not exist. The Object freely allows transport to locations where the travelling mind can incorporate without breaking local physics. However the Object will not ensure biological viability - an emotional warning is felt prior to such jumps. Other worlds may be viewed but not entered.
  • If no anchor is selected within some timeframe, the Object will either return to origin or "choose" a compatible high traffic default location. Direct links between worlds also possible prior to entering Object space (potentially induced by 2/12 test).
  • THR LMs suggest the Object maintains connection to physical body but does not send changes back until "the mind returns."
  • When travelling from the same location as a group, only one "leader" need choose a destination, and others can focus on that leader to piggyback
  • [UNREDACTED--Things can be brought back in the form of cognitive or biological alterations--UNREDACTED]

Alien World

  • LMs describe it as having four spatial dimensions, which were intuitively navigable after a momentary seizure-like episode.
  • Brief "seizure" also acclimatized/replaced physical bodies to those that were suitable to the environment. One THR stated their bodies became "the natural extension of the human form to the physics of this 4-dimensional world," and were thus still distinct from local lifeforms
  • General layout of area witnessed by LMs described as integrated flora-technology. "a futuristic city built on/in a forest, meta-materials enmeshed with trees"
  • Unclear if world existed as a "planet" in "space." City appeared to extend in all dimensions with no apparent horizon. "Gravity" felt as only local and "loose"
  • Both movement and bodies described as more "fluid," and "partly cognitive." Boundaries not absolute, some motion occuring by thought, without felt energy loss
  • Non-rigid bodies could "integrate" with other bodies or objects, either by cognitive intent or lack of cognitive focus to maintain one's form. This may include copying, swapping, summing, or other operations between two interacting bodies or objects, allowing for exchanging and transforming thoughts, personalities, memory, biophysical abilities or appearance, or "genetic" structural information.

Alien Life

  • To the extent observed by LMs, local life includes similarities to terrestial variety of hierarchically classified types, including microbial, plant-like, animal-like, etc
  • Multiple highly intelligent species, each with different physical/cognitive capacity and "cultural" roles in alien society that has well developed socio-political-economic structures
  • One dominant species (DS), notably more advanced than humans. DS bodies are more amorphous than other lifeforms- can distort size and appearance, able to produce many or no eyes, yet "see" without them in a hyperspherical range. Able to communicate using apparent telepathy via unknown medium, if any. Through direct physical contact able to more rapidly induce cognitions in others, including interpreting or bypassing symbolic language. Can body alter to have wings and fly, among other transformations.
  • Several groups of lesser intelligent species, ranging roughly in cognitive ability from a human IQ 60 to IQ 140. Bodies are more rigid, though still integrable to an extent. Each having varying attributes: strength, speed, sensory perception etc. The lowest used only gestural communication, higher ones verbal and "artistic." Most observed were primarily engaged in working class or leisure activity.
  • LMs were not able to parse most passing conversations, however concluded that while generally stable, there were tensions between different intelligent species, and possibly large conflicts between separate groups of DS members at distant locations.
  • Several LMs report a sense that there was a much larger, diffuse living organism, possibly the world itself. This may or may not be involved in allowing biological integration between living creatures and each other, or occasionally inanimate objects. It is suggested it also produces the medium for telepathy.
  • DS members reportedly communicated with members, indicating that knowledge and use of the Object was limited to more advanced species typically. Majority of life in most worlds was local nontravellers.

Hostile Life

  • Most life was benign or apathetic to LMs, however the latter claim to have encountered a group of malevolent intelligent beings, comprising multiple species, members of which are capable of travel using the Object
  • LMs state DS refer to this group as "Hunters," saying they are a known threat to travellers of all species, with the intent of expanding into and overtaking other less advanced worlds.
  • Hunters will shadow travellers or mimic the appearance of friendly/beautiful members of the target species or alien helpers.
  • Hunters attempt physical/cognitive contact to gain target language, similar to DS, then assist their them to travel, intending to join their group in abstract space and piggyback to victim homeworlds
  • LMs state they were targeted and approached by this group, only alerted when local DS intercepted and skirmish ensued.
  • Two LMs reportedly killed in this skirmish. DS were able to force Hunter retreat, then investigated 2 other LMs and noted they had already been "infected" or integrated with hunter biology, to be used as trackers.
  • Two infected LMs quarantined by DS, and eventually "extracted" from their bodies, severing connection with the Object and stranding them in alien world. DS indicated only other option was death, while extraction would only kill their bodies back in home world.
  • DS warned members that if they suspect they are being viewed by hunters, to assume they are already infected. Once in Object space, safest option is to travel to a specific world that is known to be highly mentally hostile to hunters in particular. This may force excision of hunter information, however DS recommended self-sacrifice as only safe option.

Analysis and Recommendations

Due to high consistency of independent reports, current explanations are limited to: A- premeditated collusion of members for unknown purpose, B -unexplained shared hallucination, C- entangled brain states during catatonic period, or D- veridical account provided by surviving members. D offers theoretical explanation for catatonia (cognitive departure, Object maintaining a link to the body), fever spikes (acclimatizing to alien world and incorporating cognitive updates upon return), CoD for members that DNS. C has possible physical explanation given [REDACTED], B and D are not physically verified mechanisms. A has no ascertained motivation but is the most consistent with known phenomena. Recommend permanent d/c of Project [REDACTED], continue quarantine and observation of surviving members until further event causes can be established.
